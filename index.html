Macroeconomic Context

The global economy is navigating a complex transition: moderating growth, persistent inflation, accommodative central banks, and elevated geopolitical tensions. These forces will shape sector performance through Q2 2026.

🇺🇸 United States

GDP growth at 2–3%, inflation slightly above target (~2.5%). Fed cutting rates (now 4.00–4.25%), with further 25bp cuts expected in Oct/Dec.

🇨🇳 China

Growth slowing to 4–5% amid deflationary pressures. Beijing easing aggressively—cutting rates and RRR to stimulate demand.

🇪🇺 Eurozone

Inflation near 2% target with modest ~1% growth. ECB has paused rate cuts as economy stabilizes.

🌍 Geopolitics

New U.S. tariffs and conflicts (Ukraine, Middle East) creating risk premia. Trade volumes weakening; energy security concerns rising.

Investment Recommendation

Given the macro landscape and sector dynamics, I recommend the following allocation strategy for the next 6 months:

  • 1. OVERWEIGHT Semiconductors (Primary Focus) — The AI technology boom is real and sustainable. Robust earnings, secular demand drivers, and policy support make this the highest-conviction play. Target leading chipmakers and equipment suppliers.
  • 2. SELECTIVE Commodities (Secondary Position) — Focus on industrial and precious metals as inflation hedges and precious metals plays. Accept near-term volatility for long-term structural deficits in copper, lithium, and rare earths.
  • 3. CAUTIOUS Energy (Gas/LNG Only) — Avoid broad oil exposure given supply abundance. Instead, favor U.S. natural gas producers and LNG infrastructure plays capitalizing on European demand.
  • 4. UNDERWEIGHT Transport — Stay out or maintain minimal exposure. Falling freight rates, aviation weakness, and overcapacity suggest significant downside risk.

Bottom Line: Lean aggressively into Semiconductors first, selectively add Commodities exposure, play Energy cautiously through gas/LNG, and avoid Transport entirely.
